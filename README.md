# France Road Accidents (2005 to 2016)

## Overview

An analysis of **BAAC national accident records**
([Kaggle dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/ahmedlahlou/accidents-in-france-from-2005-to-2016):
caracteristics, places, users, vehicles, holidays; 839,985 accidents, 1.88 million involved
persons, 50,589 deaths). 

## Executive summary

France has cut road deaths sharply since 2005, but the toll that remains is concentrated in
predictable times, places and people that current spending does not target. Fatality risk does
not follow traffic volume: it peaks on the empty night road, on the ordinary rural
*départemental* network, and among pedestrians, the young at weekends and the old at crossings.
A crash outside town is nearly five times as deadly as one inside it, so the priority is
**severity, not volume**.

**Recommendation:**

1. Set night and weekend enforcement windows plus night transport on hang-out routes.
2. Upgrade the deadliest *départemental* corridors with median barriers, rumble strips and 2+1 layouts.
3. Protect pedestrians with lower town speed limits, safer crossings and signal timing for an ageing population.
4. Focus and fund more on accident **severity**, not accident volume.

## Six findings that should redirect France's road safety spending

| # | Lens | Finding |
|---|------|---------|
| 1 | **WHEN** | The road kills most when it is quiet, a 6.7% death rate at 4am, 3x the evening rush. |
| 2 | **WHO (young)** | Young adults are most at risk on weekend nights, one in four deaths of 18 to 24 year-olds falls in the weekend 1 to 6am window. |
| 3 | **WHO (old)** | Seniors die as pedestrians 4x more often than adults (38% vs 9%), two thirds at or near a crossing. |
| 4 | **WHAT** | Deaths dropped for every road user except pedestrians (+5% since 2006, while car occupants fell 33%). |
| 5 | **HOW & WHY** | Two in three deaths involve a drifting vehicle or one going straight; the lane drift alone precedes one death in five. |
| 6 | **WHERE** | Rural crashes are nearly 5x deadlier than urban ones (13.6 vs 2.6 deaths per 100 crashes), the deadliest *départements* are all rural, and most rural roads are undivided two-lane *départemental* roads. |
